What Steppe stands for

Six commitments, written into the articles — not the marketing.

01
No ads. Ever.
No feeds tuned to sell your attention. The only customer is the membership.
02
No owner.
A public benefit nonprofit with no shares to buy. Steppe cannot be sold.
03
You govern it.
Members vote. Time spent earns weight, so newcomers can't be steamrolled and incumbents can't entrench.
04
Your data is yours.
Export everything, or take it and leave. Verification is required once, then never sold.
05
$4 a month.
Flat. Hardship waiver, always. Surplus goes to a Community Fund the members allocate.
06
Local.
Built for Redmond and Central Oregon. Hard cap of twelve communities, so it stays answerable.

Built so it can't be taken from you.

Steppe is a member-owned public benefit nonprofit. Its mission is entrenched in the founding articles and can't be amended away by a future board. Surplus flows to a Community Fund the members allocate by ranked-choice vote. No single funder exceeds 30%. The structure is the promise — not a terms-of-service page that changes the day incentives do.
