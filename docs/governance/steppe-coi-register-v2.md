## Purpose and scope

This register is the canonical record of disclosed conflicts and related-party relationships affecting Steppe's directors, officers, and staff. It operationalizes Steppe's Conflict of Interest Policy *[policy reference — TBD; the policy itself is not yet in the counsel packet]* and sits alongside the recusal provisions in the governing documents. A relationship is recorded here whether or not Steppe is a party to it: a private commercial relationship between two directors, or a family relationship between a director and a Steppe counterparty, is disclosable on its own terms because it can bear on board independence.

## Standing recusals

- **Cobb — Redmond Compass.** Director Holli Cobb (owner, Redmond Compass) recuses from any Steppe–Redmond Compass organizational matter, **and from any matter in which Mr. Chism or his entities have a financial interest** (see Entry 001).
- **Weimer — Herringbone Books.** Should Steppe and Herringbone Books ever enter a paid arrangement, Director Brandon Weimer recuses from any board matter touching it. Ordinary platform membership is not a conflict.
- **Weimer — Ignite Empowerment Foundation (fiscal sponsor).** Director Weimer is the spouse of IEF's founder; he recuses from every Steppe–IEF matter (see Entry 002).
- **Chism — own compensation & entities.** Director/ED Gregory Chism recuses from setting his own compensation and from any matter in which Sunbreak LLC or Three Canyon Consulting LLC has a financial interest, **including matters involving Redmond Compass or Ms. Cobb** (see Entry 001).

> **[COUNSEL]** Confirm which recusals (if any) belong in the Articles of Incorporation versus the Conflict of Interest Policy / bylaws. Entrenching individual, person-specific recusals in the Articles is rigid (directors change; Articles amendment is heavy); the policy/register layer is usually the better home.

## Entries

### 001 — Three Canyon Consulting ↔ Redmond Compass

- **Disclosing person:** Greg Chism, Founding Executive Director and director.
- **Nature:** Mr. Chism, through **Three Canyon Consulting LLC** (confirmed 2026-07-08 as the holding entity), holds a commercial engagement providing application-development services to **Redmond Compass**, whose founder, Holli Cobb, is a Steppe director. The engagement is at market rate under written contract.
- **Steppe involvement:** None. Steppe is not a party and contributes no funds, assets, or staff time.
- **Why recorded:** Two Steppe directors hold a private commercial relationship in which funds flow from a director's company (Redmond Compass) to the ED's LLC (Three Canyon). This creates an **indirect financial relationship** between Mr. Chism and Ms. Cobb: **neither is disinterested as to the other**, and neither may approve the other's interested-person transactions or the other's matters (including ED compensation, for Ms. Cobb).
- **Interaction with the no-Steppe-client rule:** The engagement is the one existing exception to Three Canyon's commitment to take no Steppe-related clients; going forward Three Canyon takes no **new** such clients, and Mr. Chism has committed to wind this engagement down (COI Disclosure Packet, Part 3, item 7).
- **Recusal terms (board-adopted):**
  - Director Chism recuses from any Steppe decision involving Redmond Compass or Ms. Cobb's businesses, and from setting his own compensation.
  - Director Cobb recuses from any Steppe decision involving Mr. Chism or his entities, including ED compensation, and from Redmond Compass matters.
  - The engagement is a private commercial matter conducted at arm's length, creating no Steppe obligation.
- **Engagement start date:** *[TBD — supply for the record]*

### 002 — Director Weimer ↔ Ignite Empowerment Foundation (fiscal sponsor)

- **Disclosing person:** Brandon Weimer, director (and, as needed, the ED on the organization's behalf).
- **Nature:** Director Weimer is the **spouse of the founder of Ignite Empowerment Foundation (IEF)**, which is **Steppe's confirmed fiscal sponsor** (sponsorship agreement pending signature). Under the arrangement, IEF holds and re-grants Steppe-directed funds and charges an administrative fee.
- **Steppe involvement:** Yes — Steppe is a party to the fiscal-sponsorship relationship with IEF.
- **Why recorded:** A family relationship between a Steppe director and the fiscal sponsor's founder bears directly on board independence and makes the Steppe–IEF relationship (agreement, admin fee, fund flows, transition off sponsorship) an **interested-person / related-party matter**. It also means Director Weimer — otherwise the board's most independent member relative to the Chism–Cobb conflict — is **not** a clean, universally disinterested director.
- **Recusal terms (board-adopted):**
  - Director Weimer recuses from any board deliberation or vote on the fiscal-sponsorship agreement, the sponsor administrative fee, fund transfers to or from IEF, or the transition off sponsorship, and is not counted toward the quorum approving them.
  - The Steppe–IEF terms are approved by the disinterested directors on arm's-length terms and documented contemporaneously.
  - Director Weimer remains available to serve as a disinterested approver of matters unrelated to IEF (e.g., ED compensation, Redmond Compass matters), subject to the board-composition note below.
- **Board-composition note:** With Chism, Cobb, and Weimer each carrying a disclosed relationship, **no current director is free of every conflict.** Steppe plans to **seat a fourth, fully independent director** to provide a clean, plural disinterested body for the most sensitive approvals.
- **Disclosed:** 2026-07-08 · **Board reviewed:** *[pending]*
