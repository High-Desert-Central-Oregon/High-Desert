**Purpose.** This packet records the conflict-of-interest disclosures of the individuals connected to Steppe's founding governance — three voting directors (Holli Cobb, Gregory Chism, Brandon Weimer) and one non-voting advisor (Angela Cruze). Part 1 is descriptive language for inclusion in the conflict-of-interest policy, the board minutes, and the attorney engagement brief. ## Part 1 — Descriptive Conflict-of-Interest Language

### 1.1 Holli Cobb — Director (voting)

Ms. Cobb owns Redmond Compass, a [for-profit] community-building enterprise, and a separate [for-profit] business providing [her additional paid services], which is a distinct entity from Redmond Compass. She serves on Steppe's board to help develop the community-facing components of the platform and of the organization. The organizational relationship between Steppe and Redmond Compass is one of shared mission and mutual encouragement toward community benefit; it is not a contractual program, and no funds, services, referrals, or co-branded promotion pass between the organizations. Steppe does not conduct promotion of local businesses as a program activity — verified local members, including business owners, simply participate on the platform on equal terms — so any benefit accruing to Redmond Compass is incidental to Steppe's exempt purpose. Because she is a voting director, Ms. Cobb is a "disqualified person" for purposes of the excess-benefit rules; she has agreed in advance to recuse herself from any board deliberation or vote on a matter in which Redmond Compass, her separate business, or any related interest is involved — **including any matter in which Mr. Chism or his entities have a financial interest** (see 1.4).

### 1.2 Gregory Chism — Founder & Executive Director designate (Director, voting)

Mr. Chism is the founder and Executive Director designate of Steppe and a voting director, and is therefore a "disqualified person." He holds two outside business interests, both disclosed here. The first is **Sunbreak LLC**, his solo freelance web and application-development practice serving his own unrelated clients. The second is **Three Canyon Consulting LLC**, a consulting practice in governance and responsible technology that he co-owns with Ms. Angela Cruze. Neither entity contracts with Steppe. Because Three Canyon's domain overlaps Steppe's, its scope is restricted: going forward it takes no **new** clients who are Steppe members, partners, or funders, and Mr. Chism recuses from any Steppe matter its work touches.

**[Confirmed 2026-07-08 — reconciled with COI Register Entry 001.]** **Three Canyon Consulting LLC currently holds a paid, market-rate engagement, under written contract, providing application-development services to Redmond Compass — Director Cobb's company.** Because money flows from Ms. Cobb's company to Mr. Chism's, **Mr. Chism and Ms. Cobb have an indirect financial relationship** and are **not** disinterested with respect to one another; each recuses from any Steppe matter in which the other, or the other's business, has a financial interest (see 1.4). This engagement is a disclosed, arm's-length commercial matter and is the **one existing exception** to the no-Steppe-related-clients restriction above; Mr. Chism will wind it down consistent with his acknowledgment (Part 3, item 7). **[COUNSEL: confirm the handling — a carve-out for the pre-existing Redmond Compass engagement plus a wind-down timeline — and the IRC §4958 consequence.]** Any future engagement of either entity by Steppe would be treated as an interested-person transaction under the conflict-of-interest policy — approved in advance by the disinterested directors on fair-market terms, with the basis documented contemporaneously — or declined. Mr. Chism's Executive Director compensation is set independently by the disinterested directors under the compensation policy (fixed-dollar ceiling with a capped annual adjustment) and is publicly disclosed; he does not participate in setting it.

### 1.3 Angela Cruze — Advisory member (non-voting)

Ms. Cruze co-owns Three Canyon Consulting LLC together with Mr. Chism, Steppe's founder and Executive Director. Precisely because she shares ownership of that practice with the Executive Director, she serves Steppe in a non-voting, advisory capacity only and does not participate in governance votes. As a non-voting advisor without other substantial influence over the organization's affairs, she is generally not a "disqualified person"; her disclosure is maintained for good-governance and transparency purposes. Any engagement of Three Canyon Consulting LLC by Steppe would be an interested-person transaction by reason of both her and Mr. Chism's ownership, handled under the policy.

### 1.4 The founder–director relationship (Chism ↔ Cobb)

Mr. Chism and Ms. Cobb collaborate outside Steppe on pro bono community-building activities and free consultation sessions, and they refer professional matters to one another. **[Revised v1.3–v1.4, 2026-07-08.]** Earlier versions of this packet treated the founder–director relationship as collaborative and non-financial and concluded, on that basis, that each of Mr. Chism and Ms. Cobb could serve as a disinterested decision-maker on the other's interested-person transactions. **That premise no longer holds.** As disclosed in the COI Register (Entry 001) and confirmed 2026-07-08, **Three Canyon Consulting LLC (Mr. Chism's practice) holds a paid, market-rate engagement with Redmond Compass, Ms. Cobb's company**, so money flows from Ms. Cobb's company to Mr. Chism's and the two directors have an **indirect financial relationship**.

Consequently, **neither is a disinterested decision-maker with respect to the other**: Mr. Chism recuses from matters involving either of his businesses, his own compensation, **and Redmond Compass or Ms. Cobb's other business**; Ms. Cobb recuses from matters involving Redmond Compass, her separate business, **and any Steppe matter in which Mr. Chism or his entities have a financial interest, including his compensation**. **Neither may act as the disinterested approver of the other's interested-person transactions.**

Because the founding board is small and its relationships overlap, the disinterested approving body must be mapped transaction by transaction (see 1.5). Director Weimer has **no** financial relationship with Mr. Chism or Ms. Cobb and can serve as a disinterested approver of the Chism–Cobb interested matters and of Executive Director compensation — **but** Mr. Weimer is himself an interested person as to Steppe's fiscal sponsor (see 1.5), and relying on a single disinterested director is thin. **Steppe therefore plans to seat a fourth, fully independent director** so that the most sensitive approvals (ED compensation and any interested-person transaction) are made by a clean, plural disinterested body. **Until such a body is in place, those approvals cannot satisfy the IRC §4958 rebuttable-presumption procedure.** A majority-independent board is strongly advisable. **[COUNSEL to confirm the §4958 analysis and the composition of the disinterested approving body for each conflict.]**

### 1.5 Brandon Weimer — Director (voting)

Mr. Weimer owns **Herringbone Books**, a local retail bookshop, and is a Steppe director. Ordinary platform membership by Herringbone Books or its owner is not a conflict; should Steppe and Herringbone Books ever enter a paid arrangement, Mr. Weimer recuses from any board matter touching it.

In addition — and material to board independence — **Mr. Weimer is the spouse of the founder of Ignite Empowerment Foundation (IEF), Steppe's confirmed fiscal sponsor.** Because of that family relationship, Mr. Weimer is an interested person with respect to the Steppe–IEF relationship and **recuses from any board deliberation or vote on the fiscal-sponsorship agreement, the sponsor administrative fee, fund transfers to or from IEF, or the transition off sponsorship** (see COI Register, Entry 002). By reason of the IEF relationship he is **not** conflicted as to Mr. Chism's compensation or Ms. Cobb's Redmond Compass matters, and may serve as a disinterested approver of those.

**Board composition.** With Mr. Chism (founder/ED, his LLCs, and the Three Canyon–Redmond Compass engagement), Ms. Cobb (Redmond Compass and that same engagement), and Mr. Weimer (Herringbone Books and the IEF spousal tie) each carrying a disclosed relationship, **no current director is free of every conflict.** Recusal can be mapped transaction by transaction, but to give Steppe a clean, plural disinterested body for its most sensitive approvals, **Steppe plans to seat a fourth director with no relationship to Mr. Chism, Ms. Cobb, IEF, or any Steppe counterparty.** **[COUNSEL: confirm the recusal map and whether a majority-independent board is required for the approvals Steppe anticipates; confirm the Steppe–IEF fiscal-sponsorship terms are approved by disinterested directors (Weimer recused) at arm's length.]**

### 1.6 Governing standards applied

- Steppe is organized and operated for public benefit; it does not operate for the benefit of private interests.
- No part of Steppe's net earnings may inure to the benefit of any insider; this prohibition is absolute.
- Any benefit to a private party, including Redmond Compass, must be incidental — a byproduct of public benefit and insubstantial in amount relative to it.
- Any transaction between Steppe and an interested person (or an entity in which an interested person has an interest) is approved in advance by the disinterested directors, supported by fair-market comparability data, and documented contemporaneously in the minutes — the rebuttable-presumption procedure under IRC §4958.
- Each interested person discloses conflicts on appointment, at least annually thereafter, and upon any material change.
- Steppe's no-advertising, member-owned-data, and reversibility commitments apply to every partnership; no outside relationship may create a dependency on, or steer Steppe toward, a business in which an insider has an interest.
