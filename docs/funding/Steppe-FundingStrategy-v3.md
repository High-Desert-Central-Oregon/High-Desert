### The Connect Infrastructure Track (2028+, deployment-shaped)

Steppe Connect — rural connectivity pilots and the Tribal Digital
Sovereignty Program — opens a distinct government lane. Every entry
follows the posture: infrastructure only, never operations, fully
disclosed, and for tribal work the Tribes lead and own everything.

  - **USDA Community Connect & Distance Learning/Telemedicine (FY2026
    open, \~$44M combined)** — deployment- and facility-shaped — viable
    only with a deployment partner (a rural utility cooperative or ISP
    builds; Steppe is community anchor). The cooperative kinship
    with Central Electric is the natural opening conversation

  - **The coalition path (join first)** — the Oregon Broadband Equity
    Coalition — OSU Extension–led, free to join, with Tribal Nations,
    providers, and government at the table — is where rural and tribal
    connectivity work is coordinated and funded. Link Oregon (network
    services to tribal/education/nonprofit sectors) and Central Electric
    Cooperative (Redmond deployment partner) are the technical allies
    met through it. This network, not a cold application, is the
    credible entry to every line below

  - **State broadband set-asides (via Oregon Broadband Office)** — the
    live channel for tribal-lands connectivity in 2026+ — the Warm
    Springs co-application pathway, with the Tribes as lead applicant
    and owner of all assets. The Warm Springs Community Action Team, a
    tribal-member-led nonprofit with grant capacity on staff, is the
    relationship through which this work eventually becomes real — on
    the Tribes’ timeline

  - **Federal tribal broadband (NTIA)** — no open window in 2026;
    awarded tribes are in buildout. Watch for reopening; if it returns,
    it is the flagship tribal-program vehicle

  - **College-led federal education awards (NSF scholarship,
    technician-education, and two-year-college programs)** — COCC and
    OSU-Cascades are developing a regional data-literacy and makerspace
    pathway funded through these institutional channels. Steppe is
    not the applicant — the honest role is named community partner:
    deployment sites, internship placements, letters of collaboration,
    and possibly a modest community-partner subaward. Value to High
    Desert: pipeline, equipment access, and credibility, not direct
    revenue

  - **Future Ready Oregon / state workforce funds (HECC)** —
    earn-and-learn money for the internship program’s expansion, in
    partnership with COCC’s apprenticeship infrastructure and East
    Cascades Works (the regional workforce board) — moderate fit, pursue
    with the college or workforce board as lead. EDCO is the bridge to
    Business Oregon and the Oregon Innovation Council for any
    economic-development framing

  - **Meta Prineville Community Action Grants (\~$1.4K–$30K observed;
    deadline \~November)** — real money with a hard geographic rule:
    Crook County 501(c)(3)s and schools. Not available to Steppe
    today; becomes live when Prineville enters petition range or via a
    Crook County partner organization — flagged for the 2027–28
    calendar, honestly conditional

## 04 — Resiliency Rules

  - Dues fund core operations, always — if every grant vanished
    tomorrow, the platform runs

  - No single funder exceeds 30% of annual revenue; government in total
    stays ≤25%

  - Individual giving is cultivated as the third leg — target ≥15% of
    revenue by 2028; a hundred locals at $50 beats one foundation at
    $5,000 for both resilience and legitimacy

  - Pipeline discipline — once eligible, keep at least three
    applications live at all times; never depend on any one decision

  - No grant whose conditions touch the foundational commitments; every
    grant and its conditions published in the quarterly transparency
    report

  - Renewal calendar — every award enters a calendar with its report
    dates and reapplication window the day it arrives

  - Partner-as-route — for the ambitious work (connectivity, the bridge
    program, tribal infrastructure), pursue funding as a subawardee,
    co-applicant, or deployment partner through an established
    organization; shared funding is faster, more eligible, and more
    credible than leading alone, and the founding team states this
    openness plainly
