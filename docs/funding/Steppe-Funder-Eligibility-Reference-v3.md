Companion to the Business Plan funding model · Eligibility, cost-mapping
& growth-stage funding, base dues $4/mo · v3 · June 14, 2026

**How to read this.** The Business Plan reserves dues for core
operations and assigns foundation grants to capacity and expansion only.
This reference puts the eligible-cost detail beneath that rule: for each
named funder, what it will and won’t pay for, then a map from Steppe’s
early budget lines to the funder best suited to each. All three primary
funders accept applications from fiscally-sponsored organizations — the
route Steppe will use through its Model C sponsor until its own
determination arrives. Figures and windows below should be reconfirmed
on each funder’s page before applying.

**Founder-pay ladder (unchanged):** $0 while a side-project → $15–25k
during significant part-time involvement → up to the $95k full-time
ceiling (3% COLA cap, set below the academic salary). Drawn first from
dues, then grant overhead (10–15%), then earned revenue; donations only
on surplus. At the locked $4 base, dues alone fund the full $95k ceiling
near \~2,300 members (\~6.5% of Redmond), so the grant-overhead and
earned-revenue layers are a bridge below that scale, not a permanent
dependence.

**Membership benchmarks:** 200 ≈ 0.5% of Redmond (near-term trust
milestone); 500 ≈ 1.3% (a 12–18-month target); 1,000 members yields
\~$48k/yr in dues; the $95k ceiling is dues-fundable near \~2,300
members (\~6.5% of Redmond); 3,500 sustained four quarters is the
founder-protection sunset (\~$168k/yr in dues, surplus to the Community
Fund).

## 4 — Sequencing & Guardrails

*The gating item is the signed fiscal-sponsorship agreement, not the
calendar.*

Three windows are open now — OCF Fall (closes June 23), Bend Foundation
summer (closes July 17), and the Roundhouse fall Open Call (Jun–Aug).
But each effectively requires the fiscal-sponsor agreement already in
hand (OCF wants it signed within the past year; Roundhouse requires
active pursuit of 501(c)(3) status). If the Ignite Empowerment
Foundation agreement is not signed within days, the June 23 OCF deadline
is too tight — and a thin application rushed without fieldwork evidence
is worse than waiting for the next cycle.

**Recommended near-term path:** lead with the two rolling, deadline-free
options that also fit Steppe’s actual readiness — the Roundhouse Small
Grant (monthly review, welcomes fiscally-sponsored orgs in pursuit of
status, and is explicitly the “get to know each other” entry) and the
Ford Family Good Neighbor grant (rolling, once the 25% match is lined
up). Use those to build a track record and relationships, then target
the OCF Fall cycle and the Roundhouse fall Open Call in the next round,
once the sponsor agreement and fieldwork evidence are both in hand.

### Guardrails

  - Keep any single grant under 30% of total revenue — size and stagger
    asks accordingly.

  - Each grant is restricted: track every award against its allowed
    budget lines, and net the sponsor’s 5–15% fee out of the usable
    total.

  - Line up Ford Family’s 25% match (cash or in-kind) before applying
    there.

  - Frame app work as accessibility, equity, and community access for
    most funders; Roundhouse is the one that will fund “program-aligned
    software” by that name.

  - Dues fund core operations and the ED salary — never a grant. This
    keeps Steppe inside both its funding model and its anti-dependency
    commitments.

**Confirm before applying.** Amounts, windows, and eligible-cost lists
change by cycle. Reconfirm each on the funder’s own page — especially
the Bend Foundation, whose public guidelines are limited. This reference
is a companion to, not a replacement for, the Business Plan funding
model.
