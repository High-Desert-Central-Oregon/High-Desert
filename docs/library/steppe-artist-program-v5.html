Not sourcing channels: Warm Springs cultural institutions

Research surfaced the Museum at Warm Springs and Tananáwit (a Confederated Tribes Native-artist collective). These are sovereign cultural institutions and are explicitly excluded from this pipeline. Any engagement with tribal artists is Tribe-led under the Digital Sovereignty framework, on the listening-posture timeline — never sourced through a gallery channel or commissioned masthead.

04

Programs

Masthead of the Month website

The Strata Seal stays constant everywhere; the hero masthead artwork rotates monthly. The generative landscape is the default, and a featured local piece takes its place for the month, credited inline and archived in a Masthead Gallery that links back to the artist. Paid commission or license; artist keeps copyright. Start every other month, move to monthly as the pipeline fills.

Township versions app

App versions align with the townships above; each carries a local artist's masthead representing that place, recruited as the version launches. The seal stays constant; the artwork localizes. Warm Springs is never a township slot (see partners note).

Flyers early build

Next print stream after the cards, same A/B QR convention (utm per variant → Plausible Signup goal, via self-owned /j and /q redirects). First run brand-spare; a later featured-artist variant uses the Masthead art. Print locally — RC Mae's Redmond shop is the first lead. Modest, editable runs until the mark resolves.

Stickers launch swag

Free swag for events plus a member welcome perk. Strata Seal die-cut, a playful "$4 a month" sticker, and artist-collab stickers — the pipeline is rich, since Sheila Dunn, Michele Michael, and Hannah Jensen already produce stickers and ISoMiMo does custom apparel. Local print, modest runs.

Launch events · Redmond

Initial release · Redmond Compass town hall soft launch

The first public moment rides along on a Redmond Compass town hall — primarily about Compass and improving local businesses, with Steppe getting a short soft-launch slot: here's what Steppe is, sign up if it speaks to you. Low-key, no hard sell. Bring the QR signup cards (Coin/Quiet) and capture emails on the /j and /q redirects so the Plausible Signup goal is live from day one.

06

When to engage artists

Now · pre-launch

Open relationships, don't commission

Warm, honest intros and event invitations — no exposure-only asks. Priority: RC Mae and Charis (Redmond anchors); confirm Sisters/Prineville/Madras candidates through their galleries.

At launch

First paid commissions

Commission the first one-to-three mastheads from Redmond artists; open the Masthead Gallery; live painting and swag at the Herringbone inaugural.

Township rollout

Recruit by place

Bring on each township's artist as its version ships, sourcing through the local gallery/arts org where we don't yet have a name.

07

How we work with artists

Pay fairly — honorarium or commission, never exposure alone.
Artists keep copyright; Steppe takes a limited, revocable display license. Credit and a link, always.
A short written agreement per piece: scope, term, fee, usage, archive.
Reciprocity — the gallery links back; we point members to buy from the artists.
COI: Greg commissions as ED inside the nonprofit, arm's-length and at market rate; documented.
Local-first, vendor-neutral printing; modest, editable runs until the ISoMiMo mark resolves.
Tribal art only under the Digital Sovereignty framework, Tribe-led — never through this pipeline.
Several roster artists share our public-lands values (Sheila Dunn, Michele Michael) — a natural fit, not a sales pitch.
