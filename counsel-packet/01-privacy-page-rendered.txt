Privacy

We have nothing to gain from your data.

Most platforms bury privacy in a menu you have to go find. Here it's
part of what Steppe is: a member-owned nonprofit that runs no ads, keeps
no trackers, and has nothing to gain from your information.

Last updated June 2026

[]

Our commitments

Six promises we keep.

a.

We never sell or share your data

There's no one to sell it to, and no business here that wants it. Steppe
runs on member dues, full stop.

b.

No advertising, no ad-tech

No third-party trackers, no advertising pixels, and no behavioral
profiles. Nothing watches what you do in order to sell you something or
to someone later.

c.

We verify you're a neighbor, then delete the proof

Membership means showing you live in the area. The moment a person
confirms it, we delete what you sent and keep only that you're verified,
the date, and the method — never an ID, a document, or your legal name.
To each other, members are known by a username. Because we don't keep
the proof, we can't produce or disclose it, even under legal process.

d.

Your profile is private by default

Every field starts hidden. You choose, one at a time, whether it's
visible to no one or to members.

e.

Messages stay inside Steppe

Conversations between neighbors stay between neighbors. We don't read
them, and we never sell or share them. We keep them only for a limited
window, and would hand them over only if valid legal process required
it.

f.

You can leave with everything

Export all of your data any time, and delete your account whenever you
want. When you go, we delete our copy.

This plain-language page is the one we actually live by. We keep only
what a verified, member-owned community needs — your account, what you
choose to post, and messages for a short window — each kept to a minimum
and shared only if valid legal process requires it. Your verification
proof isn't on that list: once we've confirmed you're a neighbor, it's
gone. There's a full legal policy with the formal detail if you want it,
and we're always glad to answer questions.
