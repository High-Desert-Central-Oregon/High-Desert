## Purpose and scope

This register is the canonical record of disclosed conflicts and related-party relationships affecting Steppe's directors, officers, and staff. It operationalizes Steppe's Conflict of Interest Policy *[policy reference — TBD]* and sits alongside the recusal provisions entrenched in the Articles of Incorporation. Entrenched recusals are constitutional and amendable only by foundational amendment; entries and policy-level recusal notes in this register are board-adopted and revisable at the policy layer.

A relationship is recorded here whether or not Steppe is a party to it. A private commercial relationship between two directors is disclosable on its own terms, because it can bear on board independence.

## Standing recusals (entrenched in the Articles)

- **Cobb — Redmond Compass partnership.** Director Holli Cobb (founder, Redmond Compass) recuses from any Board vote touching a Steppe–Redmond Compass organizational arrangement.
- **Weimer — Herringbone Books (pre-committed).** Should Steppe and Herringbone Books ever enter a paid arrangement, Director Brandon Weimer recuses from any Board vote touching it. Ordinary platform membership is not a conflict.

## Entries

### 001 — Three Canyon Consulting ↔ Redmond Compass

- **Disclosing person:** Greg Chism, Founding Executive Director and director
- **Nature:** Greg Chism, through Three Canyon Consulting LLC, holds a commercial engagement providing application-development services to Redmond Compass, whose founder, Holli Cobb, is a Steppe director. The engagement is at market rate under written contract.
- **Steppe involvement:** None. Steppe is not a party and contributes no funds, assets, or staff time.
- **Why recorded:** Two Steppe directors hold a private commercial relationship. The Articles' Compass-partnership recusal addresses Steppe↔Compass organizational matters; it does not cover this private, paid engagement, in which funds flow from a director's organization to the ED's LLC.
- **Recusal terms (board-adopted, policy-level companion to the entrenched Compass recusal):**
  - Director Cobb's standing recusal on Steppe–Redmond Compass organizational matters continues unchanged.
  - Director Chism recuses from any Steppe decision involving Redmond Compass.
  - The Three Canyon–Redmond Compass engagement is a private commercial matter between the ED and a fellow director's organization, disclosed and conducted at arm's length, creating no Steppe obligation.
  - Either director recuses where this relationship could reasonably bear on independence.
